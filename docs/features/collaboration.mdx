## Teams, rooms, and governance

Invite teammates, assign roles, and control AI spend per person or team. Shared policies define who can run agents, edit certain branches, or trigger deployments so you don’t have to track permissions manually.

### WebRTC chatrooms and pair programming

Start a chatroom from the top bar to open a WebRTC audio/video session that sticks with the workspace. Live cursors, inline messaging, and shared terminals let pairs talk through prompts while the AI answers in context.

### Inline comments, sticky notes, and context pins

Drop inline comments on files, pin symbols with descriptions, or add sticky notes to the workspace so collaborators land exactly where you left off. Pinned contexts and project bookmarks survive reloads and include links to the AI prompts or workflows you were running.

### Session recordings and playbacks

Enable session recording to capture everything the AI and team did during a pairing session. The playback includes prompts, outputs, terminal commands, and analytics so you can revisit how the assistant shaped the project, highlight a bug, or prepare a demo.

### Analytics, approvals, and support

The analytics tab shows AI spend, active users, and feature usage so you can stop wasteful experiments before they become bills. Approval roles let leads sign off on sensitive workflows (deployments, Arduino flashing) before they run, and the built-in support link (or your helpdesk) can include session links and recordings for faster triage.

<Note>
  WebRTC rooms, chatrooms, and inline notes keep the AI-centric workflow collaborative; use bookmarks and recordings to hand off work without re-explaining context.
</Note>
