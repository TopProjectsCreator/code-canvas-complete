## Arduino development from the prompt

Open the hardware panel, select your board (Uno, Nano, Mega, Leonardo, Micro, ESP32, ESP8266), and click **Compile & Upload**. The workspace handles the upload method—OTA for ESP boards, Bluetooth for supported targets—and shows success messages when the device receives the firmware.

### Scratch & block prototyping

Use the Scratch workspace to drag blocks, wire logic, and immediately see a simulation. When you are ready, press **Export to C++** and let the assistant make the text version match your block layout.

### Toolchain health

The UI checks whether the upload tools are available before you try to flash. If a required uploader is missing, the workspace shows a clear warning and a link to the recommended setup steps so you can keep prototyping.
