## Overview

The Upload panel compiles your `.ino` sketch for the selected board and transfers the binary to the physical device. It supports two transfer methods — OTA (over-the-air) for ESP boards and Bluetooth for AVR boards — without requiring any local IDE installation.

## Selecting your board

1. Open the **Hardware** panel from the sidebar
2. Click the **Board** dropdown and choose your device (Uno, Mega, ESP32, etc.)
3. The upload method is selected automatically based on the board type

## OTA upload (ESP32 / ESP8266)

Over-the-air upload works over your local Wi-Fi network:

1. Your ESP board must already have OTA support flashed (via USB the first time)
2. Enter the board's **IP address** — find it by running `Serial.println(WiFi.localIP())` in a sketch
3. Enter the **port** (default: 3232 for ESP32, 8266 for ESP8266)
4. Enter an **OTA password** if your sketch requires one
5. Click **Upload** — the binary streams over the network

Progress messages appear in the upload log. On success, the board reboots automatically.

## Bluetooth upload (AVR boards)

Bluetooth upload uses a local Arduino bridge service:

1. Pair your Bluetooth-to-serial adapter (HC-05 / HC-06) with your computer
2. Enter the **Bluetooth port** in the hardware sidebar (e.g., `/dev/cu.HC-05-SerialPort` or `COM6`)
3. Click **Upload** — the bridge connects, resets the board, and flashes using `avrdude`

## AI-assisted upload

Ask the assistant to handle the upload for you:

- **"Flash the latest build"** — compiles and triggers the upload automatically
- **"What IP address should I enter?"** — reads your sketch to find the WiFi IP print statement
- **"My upload failed, what should I do?"** — diagnoses the error and suggests fixes

## Prompt helpers

If a required uploader is missing (avrdude, esptool, espota.py), the workspace shows a clear warning with installation steps. The assistant can also:

- Auto-detect board type from your sketch's `#include` statements
- Suggest the correct baud rate for your board
- Verify the binary size before flashing

## Progress and retry

The upload shows:

- Percentage progress bar during binary transfer
- Bytes written vs. total binary size
- Final **Success** or **Error** status card

If the upload fails, click **Retry** to re-flash without re-entering your board settings. The assistant logs the error so you can ask for a diagnosis.
